Banking Essentials

Master the fundamentals of Australian banking to manage your money safely and effectively. Learn about different account types, how to choose the right bank, and essential banking skills for students.

Explore Account Types

Types of Bank Accounts

Transaction Account

Your everyday account for transactions, bill payments, and easy access to your money through EFTPOS and ATMs.

Pros:

  • Easy access to funds
  • Debit card included
  • Online banking features
  • Direct credit available
  • No transaction limits

Cons:

  • Little to no interest
  • Account keeping fees
  • Overdrawn fees
  • Minimum balance requirements

Savings Account

Build your emergency fund and save for goals while earning interest on your money with high-interest savings accounts.

Pros:

  • Earns interest
  • Government guarantee (up to $250k)
  • Encourages saving
  • Easy to open
  • Safe place for money

Cons:

  • Limited transactions
  • Variable interest rates
  • May require conditions
  • Less accessible than transaction accounts

Student Account

Special accounts designed for students with reduced fees and educational benefits, often with no account keeping fees.

Pros:

  • No account keeping fees
  • Lower minimum balance
  • Student concessions
  • Financial education resources
  • Easy approval process

Cons:

  • Age/enrolment restrictions
  • Limited features
  • Benefits may expire
  • Student ID verification required

Digital Banking Features

Modern Australian banking is digital-first. Here are essential online banking features you should know about:

Mobile Banking

Check balances, transfer money, pay bills, and manage your account using your smartphone anywhere, anytime.

Cheque Deposits

Deposit cheques instantly by taking a photo with your phone (though cheques are rarely used in Australia).

Account Alerts

Get SMS or app notifications for low balances, large transactions, bill due dates, and suspicious activity.

PayID & Osko

Send money instantly using phone numbers or email addresses through Australia's New Payments Platform.

Spending Insights

Track your spending patterns, categorise expenses, and set budgets directly in your banking app.

BPAY & Scheduled Payments

Pay bills using BPAY and set up recurring payments for rent, utilities, and other regular expenses.

Smart Banking Tips for Australian Students

Choose the Right Account

Look for student accounts with no account keeping fees, good ATM access near your campus, and digital banking features. Many banks offer fee-free accounts for students.

Set Up Direct Credit

Have your part-time job pay, Centrelink payments, or family support deposited directly into your account. It's faster and often waives fees.

Use Your Bank's ATM Network

ATM fees can be $2-3 per transaction. Stick to your bank's ATMs or choose banks like ING that reimburse all ATM fees.

Understand Account Fees

Watch out for account keeping fees, international transaction fees, and overdrawn account fees. Many can be avoided with the right account choice.

Set Up PayID

Register your mobile number or email as a PayID to receive instant transfers. It's more secure than sharing your BSB and account number.

Monitor Your Account

Check your account regularly through mobile banking. Set up alerts for all transactions to catch fraud quickly and stay on top of spending.

Account Opening Checklist

What You'll Need to Open an Australian Bank Account:

Photo ID: Australian driver's licence, passport, or proof of age card
Proof of Address: Utility bill, rental agreement, or council rates notice
Tax File Number (TFN): To avoid withholding tax on interest earned
Student ID: For student account benefits and fee waivers
Initial Deposit: Usually $10-$50 to open the account
Mobile Number: For SMS alerts and PayID registration
Parent/Guardian (if under 18): May need joint account holder or guardian consent

Government Financial Protection

All Australian banks are covered by the Government's Financial Claims Scheme, which protects deposits up to $250,000 per account holder per bank. Your money is safe with any Australian Authorised Deposit-taking Institution (ADI).

Banking Security & Safety

Protect your money and personal information with these essential security practices:

Strong Passwords & PINs

Use unique, complex passwords for online banking. Keep your PIN secret and change it regularly. Enable two-factor authentication when available.

Secure Connections

Never do banking on public Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or secure home network. Always check for "https://" in the URL.

Monitor Regularly

Check your account daily for unauthorised transactions. Set up SMS alerts for all transactions and low balances.

Scam Awareness

Banks never ask for passwords via email, SMS, or phone. If in doubt, hang up and call your bank directly using the number on your card.

ATM & EFTPOS Safety

Cover your PIN when entering it. Check for card skimmers. Use ATMs in well-lit, safe locations, preferably inside bank branches.

Keep Records

Save important banking documents securely. Shred old statements and never throw away documents with account details.

Common Banking Mistakes to Avoid

Don't fall into these expensive traps: ignoring account keeping fees, not reading terms and conditions, going overdrawn frequently, using other banks' ATMs, falling for phishing scams, or not comparing banks before choosing. Take time to understand Australian banking and shop around for the best deal!
