Accommodations for Focus & Resilience

Learn about your rights and available academic accommodations for Focus & Resilience conditions in Australian schools. Get the support you need to succeed at school while managing your Focus & Resilience.

Learn Your Rights

Understanding School Accommodations

School accommodations are adjustments made to your learning environment, assessment methods, or school routine to help you succeed academically while managing Focus & Resilience challenges. These accommodations are your legal right and are designed to give you equal opportunities to learn and demonstrate your knowledge.

Your Rights

Under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (DDA) and the Disability Standards for Education 2005, Australian schools must provide reasonable adjustments for students with disabilities, including Focus & Resilience conditions. You have the right to:

  • Equal access to all educational opportunities
  • Reasonable adjustments that help you learn and participate
  • Privacy about your Focus & Resilience condition
  • Protection from discrimination or bullying
  • Support that addresses how your condition affects your learning
  • Appeal processes if accommodations are denied

Types of School Accommodations

Accommodations are personalised based on your specific needs and how your Focus & Resilience condition impacts your school experience.

Assessment & Test Accommodations

Common Assessment Support:

  • Extra time for tests and exams (usually 25-50% more time)
  • Separate, quiet room for testing
  • Breaks during long tests or exams
  • Alternative assessment formats (oral instead of written)
  • Use of assistive technology during tests
  • Rescheduling tests during Focus & Resilience episodes
  • Permission to sit tests at a different time of day

Assignment & Deadline Support

Assignment Accommodations:

  • Extensions for assignment due dates
  • Flexibility with attendance requirements
  • Modified homework expectations
  • Alternative ways to present assignments
  • Advance notice of major assignments and tests
  • Permission to leave class if anxiety or symptoms arise
  • Reduced penalties for late work due to Focus & Resilience

Classroom Environment

Learning Environment Support:

  • Preferential seating (near exit, away from distractions)
  • Permission to use fidget tools or stress balls
  • Access to a quiet space when overwhelmed
  • Modified participation requirements
  • Advance warning of loud noises or fire drills
  • Permission to wear headphones during independent work
  • Access to class notes or recordings

Social & Behavioural Support

Social and Emotional Support:

  • Access to school counsellor or wellbeing staff
  • Modified group work arrangements
  • Safe space to go when feeling overwhelmed
  • Peer support or buddy systems
  • Modified consequences for behaviour related to Focus & Resilience
  • Social skills support and training
  • Anti-bullying protection and monitoring

Academic Support Services

Learning Support:

  • Access to learning support teachers
  • Study skills training and support
  • Organisational and time management help
  • Regular check-ins with support staff
  • Modified curriculum or subject load when appropriate
  • Career guidance and planning support
  • Transition planning for post-school pathways

Crisis & Emergency Support

Crisis Support:

  • Crisis intervention procedures and plans
  • Access to school nurse or first aid
  • Emergency contact procedures
  • Safe spaces for de-escalation
  • Modified attendance during Focus & Resilience crises
  • Coordinated support with external Focus & Resilience services
  • Re-entry support after absence for Focus & Resilience

How to Get School Accommodations

Here's how to request accommodations at your school. Your parents or carers can help with this process.

1

Talk to School Staff

Start by talking to your classroom teacher, year coordinator, or school counsellor about your Focus & Resilience challenges and how they affect your learning. They can guide you through your school's process.

2

Get Medical Documentation

You'll need a letter or report from a doctor, psychologist, or Focus & Resilience professional that explains your condition and how it impacts your school performance.

3

Meet with School Team

The school will arrange a meeting with you, your parents/carers, and relevant school staff to discuss your needs and develop an accommodation plan.

4

Create Your Plan

Together, you'll create an Individual Education Plan (IEP), Individual Learning Plan (ILP), or similar document that outlines your specific accommodations and support strategies.

5

Share with Teachers

Your plan will be shared with all your teachers so they understand your needs and can provide appropriate support in their classes.

6

Regular Reviews

Your accommodation plan will be reviewed regularly (usually each term or semester) to make sure it's still meeting your needs and make any necessary changes.

What Documentation You Need

To get accommodations, you'll need proper documentation that explains your Focus & Resilience condition and how it affects your learning.

Healthcare Provider

A letter from a GP, psychiatrist, psychologist, or Focus & Resilience social worker who has diagnosed or is treating your condition.

Current Information

The documentation should be recent (usually within 12 months) and reflect your current Focus & Resilience status and needs.

Detailed Assessment

Should include your diagnosis, main symptoms, how the condition affects your daily life and learning, and recommended accommodations.

Treatment Information

Information about current treatment, medications (if any), and how these might impact your school performance or needs.

School Impact

Clear explanation of how your condition affects your ability to learn, concentrate, complete work, interact socially, or manage stress.

Recommendations

Specific suggestions from your healthcare provider about what accommodations or support would help you succeed at school.

Tips for Making Accommodations Work

Getting accommodations approved is just the beginning. Here's how to make the most of your support.

Communicate Openly

Talk to your teachers about your needs. Let them know what's working and what isn't so they can adjust their support.

Plan Ahead

Request accommodations like extensions or alternative test arrangements as early as possible, not at the last minute.

Keep Records

Keep copies of your accommodation plan, medical documentation, and any communication about your support needs.

Build Relationships

Get to know your teachers, year coordinator, and school counsellor. They're there to help you succeed.

Speak Up for Changes

If your needs change or your current accommodations aren't working, ask for your plan to be reviewed and updated.

Know Your Rights

Understand what support you're entitled to and don't be afraid to advocate for yourself if accommodations aren't being provided.

NAPLAN and External Exam Support

Students with Focus & Resilience conditions can also receive accommodations for:

  • NAPLAN: Extra time, rest breaks, separate room, or alternative arrangements
  • Year 12 External Exams (HSC, VCE, QCE, etc.): Special provisions including extra time, rest breaks, separate supervision
  • Alternative Assessment: In some cases, alternative ways to demonstrate knowledge and skills
  • Illness and Misadventure: Special consideration if Focus & Resilience impacts performance on the day

Your school will help you apply for these accommodations through the relevant state education authority.

Getting Help with School Accommodations

If you need help understanding or getting school accommodations:

Start with your school: Talk to your classroom teacher, year coordinator, deputy principal, or school counsellor

School Student Support Services: Most schools have dedicated staff for student wellbeing and learning support

If you need more help: Contact your state education department's student support services
