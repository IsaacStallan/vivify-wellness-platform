Skull Crusher

A powerful isolation exercise for building stronger, more defined triceps and improving pushing strength

Triceps (All Three Heads)
Intermediate
EZ Bar, Dumbbells, or Barbell

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscle: Triceps Brachii (All Three Heads)
  • Secondary Muscles: Deltoids (Stabilizers)
  • Equipment: EZ Curl Bar, Straight Barbell, or Dumbbells
  • Type: Isolation
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
  • Mechanics: Single Joint
  • Force: Push (Elbow Extension)

How to Perform the Skull Crusher

  1. 1

    Setting Up

    Lie flat on a bench with your feet planted firmly on the floor. If using an EZ bar or straight barbell, grip the bar with hands positioned shoulder-width apart. If using dumbbells, hold them with a neutral grip (palms facing each other).

  2. 2

    Starting Position

    Extend your arms straight up over your chest, with the bar or dumbbells directly above your shoulders. Your elbows should be fully extended but not locked. Keep your upper arms perpendicular to the floor and maintain this position throughout the exercise.

  3. 3

    Stabilize Your Position

    Engage your core muscles to stabilize your spine and keep your shoulder blades retracted and pressed into the bench. Your wrists should be straight, not bent backward, to prevent strain.

  4. 4

    Lowering Phase

    Inhale and slowly bend your elbows to lower the weight toward your forehead (hence the name "skull crusher"). Keep your upper arms stationary and perpendicular to the floor. Your elbows should point forward, not flare outward.

  5. 5

    Bottom Position

    Lower the weight until the bar is just above your forehead (or the dumbbells are on either side of your head). At this position, your elbows should be bent at approximately 90 degrees. Pause briefly at the bottom.

  6. 6

    Extension Phase

    Exhale and extend your elbows to push the weight back up to the starting position. Focus on contracting your triceps to perform the movement. Keep your upper arms stationary throughout this phase.

  7. 7

    Top Position

    Return to the starting position with arms extended directly over your chest. Squeeze your triceps at the top of the movement for a brief moment to maximize muscle activation.

  8. 8

    Repeat

    Perform the desired number of repetitions with controlled form. A typical range for skull crushers is 8-12 reps per set. When your set is complete, carefully return the weight to the starting position and then set it aside or rack it.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Upper arm position: Keep your upper arms perpendicular to the floor and stationary throughout the entire movement. Only your forearms should move.
  • Elbow alignment: Point your elbows forward, not outward, to better isolate the triceps and reduce shoulder stress.
  • Controlled movement: Perform both the lowering and lifting phases in a slow, controlled manner. Avoid using momentum.
  • Range of motion: Lower the weight until you feel a stretch in your triceps, but not so far that it causes elbow pain or shoulder movement.
  • Wrist position: Keep your wrists straight and firm throughout the exercise to prevent strain and maintain proper force transfer.
  • Breathing: Inhale during the lowering phase and exhale during the extension phase.
  • Choose the right weight: Start with a lighter weight to master the form before progressing. This exercise puts significant stress on the elbow joint.
  • Head position: Keep your head in contact with the bench throughout the movement to maintain stability.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Moving upper arms: Allowing your upper arms to drift backward or forward during the movement reduces triceps engagement and may strain your shoulders.
  • Flaring elbows: Pointing elbows outward places unnecessary stress on the shoulder joints and reduces triceps activation.
  • Using momentum: Swinging or using momentum to lift the weight reduces muscle engagement and increases injury risk.
  • Excessive weight: Using too heavy weight often leads to poor form, reduced range of motion, and potential injury to the elbows.
  • Lowering too far: Bringing the weight too far back beyond your head can place excessive stress on the elbow joint and shoulder.
  • Wrist hyperextension: Allowing your wrists to bend backward puts strain on the wrist joints and reduces force transfer to the triceps.
  • Rushing the movement: Performing the exercise too quickly reduces time under tension and triceps development.

Skull Crusher Variations

Dumbbell Skull Crusher

Using dumbbells instead of a barbell allows for a more natural movement pattern and unilateral development.

Incline Skull Crusher

Performed on an incline bench to increase the stretch on the triceps and emphasize the long head.

Decline Skull Crusher

Performed on a decline bench to change the angle of resistance and target the triceps differently.

Cable Skull Crusher

Using a cable machine provides constant tension throughout the movement for increased muscle activation.
