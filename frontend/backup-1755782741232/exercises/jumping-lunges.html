Jumping Lunges

A challenging plyometric exercise that builds lower body power, balance, and cardiovascular fitness

Lower Body, Cardio
Intermediate-Advanced
No Equipment

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscles: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings
  • Secondary Muscles: Calves, Core, Hip Flexors
  • Equipment: None
  • Type: Plyometric, Unilateral
  • Experience Level: Intermediate-Advanced
  • Mechanics: Compound
  • Force: Push (Lower Body), Explosive

How to Perform Jumping Lunges

  1. 1

    Starting Position

    Begin in a split stance with your right foot forward and left foot back, positioning yourself in a lunge. Your right knee should be directly above your right ankle, and your left knee should hover just above the floor.

  2. 2

    Proper Posture

    Keep your torso upright, core engaged, and shoulders back. Your weight should be evenly distributed between both legs, with a slight forward lean from the ankles, not the waist.

  3. 3

    Prepare to Jump

    Lower slightly deeper into the lunge position, engaging your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. Swing your arms back slightly to help generate momentum for the jump.

  4. 4

    Explosive Jump

    Powerfully push through both feet to propel your body upward, extending your hips, knees, and ankles. Simultaneously swing your arms upward to assist with the jump. Your feet should leave the ground completely.

  5. 5

    Switch Legs Mid-Air

    While in the air, switch your leg positions so that your left leg moves forward and your right leg moves backward. Keep your core tight to maintain balance during this transition.

  6. 6

    Land Softly

    Land softly in the lunge position with your left foot forward and right foot back. Absorb the impact by bending at the knees and hips, maintaining proper alignment with your left knee tracking over your toes.

  7. 7

    Continuous Movement

    Without pausing at the bottom, immediately prepare for the next repetition by lowering slightly further into the lunge and repeating the jumping and switching motion. Continue alternating legs for the prescribed number of repetitions.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Proper warm-up: Due to the high-impact nature of jumping lunges, thoroughly warm up your lower body with dynamic stretches and lighter exercises before beginning.
  • Focus on alignment: Maintain proper knee alignment by ensuring your front knee tracks directly over your ankle, not beyond your toes, to protect your joints.
  • Control the landing: Land as softly as possible through the balls of your feet first, then rolling to midfoot and finally heel, absorbing impact with bent knees.
  • Maintain torso position: Keep your chest up and shoulders back throughout the exercise. Avoid excessive forward lean which can strain your lower back.
  • Use your arms: Coordinate arm movements with your jumps to generate momentum and maintain balance. Swing arms back as you descend and forward as you jump.
  • Engage your core: Keep your abdominals braced throughout the movement to protect your spine and maintain stability during the leg switch.
  • Start with height, not speed: Focus on achieving proper height in your jump before increasing speed. Quality movements are more beneficial than quick, sloppy repetitions.
  • Breathe rhythmically: Establish a breathing pattern that works for you, such as exhaling during the explosive jumping phase and inhaling during the landing phase.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Allowing front knee to collapse inward: This places excessive stress on the knee joint. Focus on driving the knee outward in line with your toes.
  • Front foot positioning too far forward: An excessively long stride reduces power and increases knee strain. Keep your front foot at a distance that allows your knee to remain over your ankle.
  • Leaning too far forward: Excessive torso lean shifts weight distribution and can strain the lower back. Maintain an upright posture with a slight forward angle from the ankles.
  • Landing with straight legs: This creates jarring impact on your joints. Always land with bent knees to properly absorb force.
  • Incomplete leg switch: Not fully transitioning legs during the jump diminishes the exercise's effectiveness. Ensure a complete switch of front and back legs with each repetition.
  • Minimal jump height: Barely leaving the ground reduces the plyometric benefit. Focus on achieving appropriate height in your jumps through proper extension.
  • Poor landing mechanics: Slapping feet down creates excessive impact. Practice landing quietly and controlled to protect your joints.

Jumping Lunge Variations

Split Squat Jumps

A modified version where you jump vertically but return to the same leg forward position, rather than switching legs, reducing the coordination challenge.

Weighted Jumping Lunges

Adds external resistance through dumbbells or a weighted vest to increase strength demands and intensity.

Curtsy Jumping Lunges

Incorporates a curtsy lunge (crossing the back leg behind the front leg) to target the glutes and outer thighs more intensely.

Low-Impact Alternating Lunges

A beginner-friendly version where you step between lunge positions rather than jumping, maintaining the same movement pattern with reduced impact.
