Australian Tax Basics for Students

Understanding how Australian income tax works is essential for every working student.

2024-25 Australian Income Tax Rates

Taxable Income Tax Rate Tax on This Income
$0 – $18,200 0% Nil (Tax-Free Threshold)
$18,201 – $45,000 19% 19c for each $1 over $18,200
$45,001 – $120,000 32.5% $5,092 plus 32.5c for each $1 over $45,000
$120,001 – $180,000 37% $29,467 plus 37c for each $1 over $120,000
$180,001+ 45% $51,667 plus 45c for each $1 over $180,000

Good News for Students!

Most students working part-time earn under $18,200 per year, which means you pay NO income tax and likely qualify for a full refund of any tax withheld by your employer.

Student Tax Benefits & Deductions

Education Expense Offset

Up to $250

Get a tax offset for eligible education expenses including textbooks, stationery, computer equipment, and trade tools.

Requirements:

  • Expenses must be for eligible education
  • Keep all receipts and records
  • Total expenses must exceed $250
  • Cannot claim items provided by employer

Self-Education Deductions

No Limit

Deduct expenses for education that relates to your current work or is likely to increase your income.

Eligible Expenses:

  • Course fees and tuition
  • Textbooks and course materials
  • Computer equipment and software
  • Travel to and from place of education
  • Home office expenses for study

Work-Related Car Expenses

Variable

Claim car expenses for work-related travel, excluding normal home-to-work commuting.

Methods Available:

  • Cents per kilometre (up to 5,000km)
  • Logbook method (for detailed records)
  • Must keep records of work-related trips
  • Cannot claim personal use

Home Office Expenses

80c/hour

Claim running expenses for working or studying from home using the ATO's shortcut method.

Covers:

  • Electricity and gas
  • Phone and internet expenses
  • Cleaning and stationery
  • Keep records of hours worked from home

Uniform & Protective Clothing

Full Cost

Deduct the cost of compulsory work uniforms, protective clothing, and conventional clothing with logos.

Eligible Items:

  • Compulsory company uniforms
  • Protective equipment (safety boots, hard hats)
  • Conventional clothing with company logos
  • Cleaning and maintenance costs

Tools & Equipment

Variable

Claim tools and equipment needed for work, including depreciation for expensive items.

Rules:

  • Items under $300: immediate deduction
  • Items over $300: depreciate over several years
  • Must be used primarily for work
  • Keep purchase receipts

How to Lodge Your Tax Return

Follow these steps to complete your Australian tax return accurately and on time.

1

Gather Your Documents

Collect your payment summaries, receipts, and any other relevant tax documents before you start.

2

Get a Tax File Number (TFN)

If you don't have a TFN, apply online through the ATO website. You'll need this to lodge your return.

3

Choose Filing Method

Use myTax (free ATO online system), hire a tax agent, or use commercial tax software.

4

Complete Your Return

Enter your income, claim eligible deductions, and double-check all information for accuracy.

5

Lodge Before Deadline

Submit your return by 31 October. If using a tax agent, you may get an extension until May.

6

Receive Your Refund

Most refunds are processed within 2 weeks and deposited directly into your nominated bank account.

Essential Tax Documents for Students

Make sure you have these documents before starting your tax return.

Payment Summary

Your employer should provide a payment summary showing your total income and tax withheld for the financial year (1 July to 30 June).

Tip: If you don't receive a payment summary by 14 July, contact your employer or check ATO online services for pre-filled information.

Receipts & Records

Keep all receipts for work-related expenses, education costs, and other deductible items. Digital photos of receipts are acceptable.

Tip: Use the ATO app to record and store receipts throughout the year. You must keep records for 5 years.

Education Statements

Statements from your education provider showing fees paid, including HECS-HELP, FEE-HELP, or upfront payments.

Tip: Check your myGov account for education payment information that may be pre-filled in your tax return.

Bank Statements

Statements showing interest earned on savings accounts, term deposits, or other financial investments.

Tip: Even small amounts of interest must be declared. Most banks report this information to the ATO automatically.

Government Payments

Centrelink payment summaries for Youth Allowance, Austudy, or other government benefits received.

Tip: Some Centrelink payments are taxable, while others aren't. Check your Centrelink online account for payment summaries.

Investment Documents

Dividend statements, managed fund distributions, and any other investment income documents.

Tip: Keep track of franking credits on dividend payments - these can increase your refund significantly.

Expert Tax Tips for Students

Lodge Early for Faster Refunds

Lodge your return as soon as possible after 1 July to get your refund faster. The ATO processes early returns first, and you could receive your refund within 2 weeks.

Keep Digital Records

Use the ATO app to photograph and store receipts throughout the year. Digital records are just as valid as paper receipts and much harder to lose.

Claim What You're Entitled To

Don't miss out on legitimate deductions. If you incur an expense to earn your income, it's likely deductible. When in doubt, keep the receipt and ask a tax professional.

Understand HECS-HELP

If you have a HECS-HELP debt, you'll need to start making repayments once your income exceeds $51,550 (2024-25). These are automatically deducted through PAYG.

Check Pre-filled Information

The ATO pre-fills much of your tax return with information from employers and banks. Always verify this information is correct before lodging.

Consider Professional Help

If you have complex circumstances, rental properties, or significant investments, consider using a registered tax agent. Their fees are often tax-deductible.

Important Tax Reminders

Always provide accurate information in your tax return. The ATO has sophisticated data matching systems and penalties for false or misleading information can be severe. Keep all records for 5 years, and remember that working holiday makers and foreign residents have different tax rates and thresholds.
