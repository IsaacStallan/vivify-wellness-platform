Types of Networking Events

Career Fairs

Meet multiple employers in one location, learn about job opportunities, and submit resumes directly.

  • Multiple companies in one venue
  • On-the-spot interviews possible
  • Great for exploring different industries
  • Usually free for students

Industry Meetups

Informal gatherings of professionals in specific industries to share knowledge and connect.

  • Casual, friendly atmosphere
  • Learn about industry trends
  • Meet peers and mentors
  • Often include food and drinks

Professional Conferences

Large-scale events with speakers, workshops, and extensive networking opportunities.

  • Industry leaders as speakers
  • Latest trends and innovations
  • Structured networking sessions
  • Student discounts often available

Workshops & Skills Sessions

Hands-on learning experiences that also provide networking opportunities with instructors and peers.

  • Learn practical skills
  • Small group interactions
  • Expert instructors
  • Portfolio building opportunities

Alumni Networks

Connect with graduates from your university who are established in their careers.

  • Shared university connection
  • Mentorship opportunities
  • Industry insights
  • Job referral possibilities

Startup & Innovation Events

Dynamic events featuring entrepreneurs, investors, and innovation-focused professionals.

  • Entrepreneurial mindset
  • Pitch competitions
  • Innovation showcases
  • Investor connections

Networking Event Success Tips

Before the Event

Research attendees on LinkedIn, prepare your elevator pitch, bring business cards or have digital contact info ready, and set specific goals for connections you want to make.

During the Event

Arrive early when people are more approachable, ask open-ended questions, listen more than you talk, and focus on how you can help others rather than just what they can do for you.

After the Event

Follow up within 24-48 hours with a personalized message, connect on LinkedIn with a note about where you met, and suggest specific ways to continue the relationship.

Conversation Starters

"What brings you to this event?" "What trends are you seeing in your industry?" "What's the most exciting project you're working on?" Remember to share your own insights too.

Professional Presence

Dress appropriately for the event type, maintain good eye contact and posture, put your phone away during conversations, and carry yourself with confidence.

Authentic Connections

Be genuinely interested in others, find common ground beyond work, be yourself rather than trying to impress, and focus on building relationships, not collecting contacts.
