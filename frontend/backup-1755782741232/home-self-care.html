Independent Living Skills

Essential Life Skills

Laundry & Clothing Care

  1. Sort clothes by colour (whites, lights, darks) and fabric type
  2. Check pockets for coins, tissues, or important items
  3. Read care labels - they tell you water temperature and settings
  4. Measure detergent (1-2 caps for standard load)
  5. Load clothes loosely - don't overpack the machine
  6. Select appropriate water temperature (cold for darks, warm for colours, hot for whites)
  7. Start the wash cycle and set a timer
  8. Transfer to dryer immediately or hang to air dry
  9. Clean lint filter before each dryer load
  10. Fold clothes promptly to prevent wrinkles
  • Wash new dark clothes separately first time to prevent colour bleeding
  • Turn graphic tees and jeans inside out to preserve colours
  • Use cold water for most loads - saves energy and prevents shrinking
  • Don't overload - clothes need room to move and get clean
  • Remove stains ASAP - treat with cold water and stain remover
  • Air dry delicates, bras, and workout clothes to make them last longer
  • Fold or hang clothes immediately to avoid wrinkles

Cleaning & Tidying

  1. Make bed and pick up clutter first - makes room look instantly better
  2. Dust surfaces from top to bottom (dust falls down)
  3. Wipe down surfaces with appropriate cleaner
  4. Vacuum or sweep floors, including under furniture
  5. Mop hard floors with floor cleaner
  6. Clean bathroom: toilet, sink, shower/bath, mirror
  7. Take out rubbish and recycling
  8. Do dishes or load dishwasher
  9. Wipe down kitchen benches and stove
  10. Put everything back in its place
  • Clean for 15 minutes daily rather than 3 hours weekly
  • Always clean from top to bottom, inside to outside
  • Use microfiber cloths - they trap dirt better than paper towels
  • Keep cleaning supplies in a caddy for easy transport
  • Open windows for ventilation when using chemical cleaners
  • Clean spills immediately to prevent stains and sticky buildup
  • Make cleaning fun with music or podcasts

Cooking & Meal Prep

  1. Read the entire recipe before starting
  2. Wash your hands and clean work surfaces
  3. Gather all ingredients and measure them out
  4. Prep ingredients: wash, chop, dice as needed
  5. Heat pans/oven to correct temperature before cooking
  6. Cook according to recipe, tasting as you go
  7. Use a timer for accuracy
  8. Check food is cooked through (especially meat)
  9. Let food rest if recipe calls for it
  10. Clean as you cook to make cleanup easier
  • Start with simple recipes and gradually build skills
  • Meal prep on Sundays - cook grains, chop vegetables
  • Keep basic pantry staples: rice, pasta, canned beans, spices
  • Taste food throughout cooking and adjust seasoning
  • Learn knife skills - makes cooking faster and safer
  • Cook extra servings for leftovers
  • Keep a well-stocked spice rack for flavour variety

Personal Hygiene & Grooming

  1. Shower daily or every other day with warm water
  2. Wash hair 2-3 times per week (or as needed for your hair type)
  3. Brush teeth twice daily for 2 minutes each time
  4. Floss daily to remove plaque between teeth
  5. Use deodorant/antiperspirant daily
  6. Trim nails regularly and keep them clean
  7. Wash hands frequently, especially before eating
  8. Moisturise skin to prevent dryness
  9. Change underwear and socks daily
  10. Maintain good posture and grooming habits
  • Establish a consistent morning and evening routine
  • Use lukewarm water for showers - hot water dries out skin
  • Choose products suited to your skin and hair type
  • Replace toothbrush every 3 months or after illness
  • Wash face gently twice daily to prevent breakouts
  • Don't share personal hygiene items with others
  • Stay hydrated - it helps your skin and overall health

Basic Home Maintenance

  1. Learn where your fuse box and water shut-off valve are located
  2. Test smoke detector batteries monthly
  3. Clean out dryer lint trap after each use
  4. Change air fresheners and clean air vents regularly
  5. Know how to unclog drains with plunger or drain snake
  6. Keep basic tools handy: screwdriver, hammer, wrench
  7. Report maintenance issues to landlord/property manager promptly
  8. Clean gutters and outdoor areas if applicable
  9. Check and replace light bulbs as needed
  10. Maintain heating/cooling systems according to manual
  • Keep a basic toolkit for simple repairs and adjustments
  • YouTube is your friend for learning basic repair techniques
  • Know your rental/lease responsibilities vs landlord's
  • Take photos of any damage when moving in
  • Don't attempt major electrical or plumbing work yourself
  • Keep important contact numbers handy (landlord, emergency services)
  • Regular maintenance prevents bigger problems later

Organization & Storage

  1. Designate a specific place for everything you own
  2. Sort items into categories: keep, donate, toss
  3. Use vertical space with shelving and hanging organizers
  4. Store frequently used items in easily accessible places
  5. Label containers and shelves for easy identification
  6. Implement the "one in, one out" rule to prevent clutter
  7. Make your bed daily - starts the day with accomplishment
  8. Do a 10-minute tidy up each evening
  9. Organize paperwork with a simple filing system
  10. Regularly declutter - donate items you haven't used in 6 months
  • Start small - organize one drawer or area at a time
  • Use clear containers so you can see what's inside
  • Keep surfaces clear - everything should have a home
  • Digitize documents when possible to reduce paper clutter
  • Use a calendar or planner to stay organized with commitments
  • Set aside 15 minutes weekly for maintenance organization
  • Involve roommates in common area organization systems

Australian Living Tips

🏠 Rental Property Responsibilities

In Australia, tenants are typically responsible for basic cleaning, minor maintenance, and reporting issues promptly. Landlords handle major repairs, structural issues, and appliance replacement. Always document issues in writing to your property manager.

💰 Where to Shop for Supplies

Kmart and Big W for affordable homewares and cleaning supplies. Woolworths and Coles for grocery staples. Bunnings for tools and garden supplies. Check out op shops (charity stores) for cheap kitchenware and furniture.

🌡️ Climate Considerations

Australia's varied climate affects your routine - use fans and light clothing in hot weather, ensure good ventilation in humid areas, and adjust laundry drying times based on weather. Many homes don't have central heating/cooling, so plan accordingly.

♻️ Waste Management

Learn your local council's recycling rules - they vary by area. Most areas have separate bins for general waste, recycling, and green waste. Some councils offer bulk waste collection days for large items.

🚰 Water Conservation

Many Australian areas have water restrictions. Take shorter showers, fix leaks promptly, and consider water-efficient appliances. Some states offer rebates for water-saving devices.

🏥 Health & Safety

Keep a basic first aid kit handy. Know your local emergency services numbers. If renting, ensure smoke detectors work and know evacuation routes. Australia's 000 number works for police, fire, and ambulance.
