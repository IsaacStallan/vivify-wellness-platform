Dumbbell Pullover

A classic exercise that targets both the back and chest muscles while improving shoulder mobility

Lats, Serratus Anterior, Chest
Intermediate
Dumbbell, Bench

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscle: Latissimus Dorsi (Lats)
  • Secondary Muscles: Serratus Anterior, Pectoralis Major, Triceps, Teres Major
  • Equipment: Dumbbell, Bench
  • Type: Compound
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
  • Mechanics: Pull
  • Force: Pull (Arc)

How to Perform the Dumbbell Pullover

  1. 1

    Equipment Setup

    Place a flat bench in an open area with enough space to perform the movement safely. Select an appropriate dumbbell—start lighter than you think you need, as this exercise can be challenging when performed correctly.

  2. 2

    Starting Position

    Lie on the bench with your upper back and shoulders supported, feet planted firmly on the floor. Your head can be on or slightly off the bench. Hold a dumbbell with both hands, gripping the plates or the inner sides of the handle, with your arms extended over your chest.

  3. 3

    Proper Grip and Arm Position

    Your hands should be positioned under the top plate of the dumbbell (if using a standard dumbbell) or around the handle. Keep a slight bend in your elbows—they should not be completely locked out—and maintain this same bend throughout the entire movement.

  4. 4

    Lowering Phase

    Inhale as you slowly lower the dumbbell in an arc motion behind your head, keeping your core engaged and lower back in contact with the bench. Lower the weight until you feel a stretch in your lats and chest, typically when the dumbbell is approximately parallel to the floor or slightly lower.

  5. 5

    Stretched Position

    At the bottom of the movement, your arms should be extended behind your head with the weight at or slightly below bench level. Feel the stretch across your lats, serratus, and chest muscles. Maintain the slight bend in your elbows and keep your core engaged to protect your lower back.

  6. 6

    Lifting Phase

    Exhale as you use your lats and chest to pull the dumbbell back up in the same arc motion, returning to the starting position above your chest. Focus on using your lats to initiate the movement rather than relying solely on your arms.

  7. 7

    Contracted Position

    At the top of the movement, the dumbbell should be directly over your chest with your arms extended but still maintaining that slight bend in the elbows. Avoid letting the dumbbell drift too far forward toward your abdomen.

  8. 8

    Repeat

    Complete the desired number of repetitions, focusing on a controlled movement throughout the entire range of motion. When finished, carefully bring the dumbbell back to your chest and then to your thighs before sitting up.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Maintain elbow position: Keep the same degree of bend in your elbows throughout the entire movement to reduce stress on the elbow joints and maintain tension on the target muscles.
  • Control the weight: Use a controlled tempo, especially on the eccentric (lowering) phase to maximize the stretch and muscle engagement.
  • Protect your lower back: Keep your core engaged and your lower back in contact with the bench throughout the movement to prevent arching.
  • Breathe properly: Inhale during the lowering phase and exhale during the lifting phase.
  • Focus on the stretch and squeeze: Pay attention to the stretch at the bottom of the movement and the contraction of your lats and chest muscles at the top.
  • Start light: Begin with a lighter weight than you think you need to master the movement pattern before progressing to heavier weights.
  • Range of motion: Find the range of motion that gives you a good stretch without compromising form or causing shoulder discomfort.
  • Hand position options: You can hold the dumbbell by the plates or by the handle—experiment to find which position feels more comfortable and allows better control.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Arching the lower back: Allowing your lower back to excessively arch off the bench can lead to strain and injury. Keep your core engaged and maintain contact between your lower back and the bench.
  • Using momentum: Swinging the weight or using momentum reduces the effectiveness of the exercise and increases injury risk. Control the weight throughout the entire range of motion.
  • Changing elbow angle: Straightening your arms during the movement or significantly changing the elbow angle alters the mechanics of the exercise and can place undue stress on the shoulder joints.
  • Lowering too far: Lowering the weight excessively behind your head can strain your shoulders if you lack adequate mobility. Only go as far as your shoulder flexibility comfortably allows.
  • Using too heavy weight: Selecting a weight that's too heavy often leads to compromised form, reduced range of motion, and increased risk of injury.
  • Lifting the hips: Raising your hips off the bench changes the angle of pull and reduces the effectiveness of the exercise while potentially straining your lower back.
  • Rushing the movement: Performing the exercise too quickly reduces time under tension and doesn't allow for a proper stretch or contraction of the target muscles.

Dumbbell Pullover Variations

Stability Ball Pullover

Performing the exercise with your upper back supported on a stability ball to increase core engagement and range of motion.

Single-Arm Dumbbell Pullover

Working one arm at a time to address muscle imbalances and increase the anti-rotation challenge for the core.

Incline Dumbbell Pullover

Performing the exercise on an incline bench to alter the angle of pull and place more emphasis on the upper lats.

Cable Pullover

Using a cable machine instead of a dumbbell to provide constant tension throughout the movement.
