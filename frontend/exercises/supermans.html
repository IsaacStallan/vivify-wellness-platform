Supermans

A powerful posterior chain exercise to strengthen the lower back, glutes, and shoulders while counteracting poor posture

Lower Back, Glutes, Shoulders
Beginner-Intermediate
No Equipment

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscles: Erector Spinae, Glutes, Posterior Deltoids
  • Secondary Muscles: Hamstrings, Trapezius, Rhomboids
  • Equipment: None (Bodyweight Exercise)
  • Type: Dynamic, Bodyweight
  • Experience Level: Beginner-Intermediate
  • Force Type: Extension (Posterior Chain)
  • Benefits: Back Strength, Posture Improvement, Spine Health

How to Perform Supermans

  1. 1

    Starting Position

    Lie face down on a mat with your arms extended straight in front of you (like Superman flying), palms facing down. Your legs should be straight and together with toes pointed. Rest your forehead on the mat to maintain a neutral neck position.

  2. 2

    Engage Your Core

    Before lifting, gently engage your core by drawing your navel slightly toward your spine. This creates stability and supports your lower back during the movement.

  3. 3

    Lift Arms and Legs

    Simultaneously lift your arms, chest, and legs off the floor by engaging your back muscles, glutes, and shoulders. Aim to raise your limbs about 4-8 inches off the ground, depending on your current flexibility and strength.

  4. 4

    Peak Position

    At the top of the movement, your body should form a gentle arc with only your abdomen and hips in contact with the floor. Ensure your neck remains in a neutral position with your gaze directed slightly forward and down, not looking up excessively.

  5. 5

    Hold Briefly

    Hold this extended position for 2-3 seconds, focusing on squeezing your glutes and back muscles rather than just lifting higher. Think about creating length through your arms and legs, reaching in opposite directions.

  6. 6

    Lower with Control

    Slowly lower your arms, chest, and legs back to the starting position in a controlled manner. Avoid simply dropping back to the floor, which reduces the effectiveness of the exercise.

  7. 7

    Brief Rest

    Pause briefly in the starting position, allowing your muscles a momentary rest while maintaining engagement. This isn't a full relaxation but rather a reset before the next repetition.

  8. 8

    Complete Repetitions

    Repeat the movement for the prescribed number of repetitions (typically 8-15 for beginners, building to 15-20 as strength improves). Focus on quality over quantity, especially for beginners.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Optimal height: Don't aim to lift your limbs as high as possible. A moderate lift (4-8 inches) with proper muscle engagement is more effective than an excessive arch that can strain your lower back.
  • Breath control: Breathe normally throughout the movement, typically exhaling as you lift and inhaling as you lower. Avoid holding your breath, which can create unnecessary tension.
  • Arm position: While the classic Superman has arms extended straight ahead, you can also position them at a 45-degree angle (like a "Y" shape) to target the mid-back muscles more effectively.
  • Neck alignment: Keep your neck in a neutral position by looking down at the mat or slightly forward, rather than lifting your head excessively, which can strain the cervical spine.
  • Glute emphasis: Consciously squeeze your glutes during the lift to enhance posterior chain engagement and reduce excessive lower back strain.
  • Tempo control: Moving slowly and deliberately (2-3 seconds up, 2-3 second hold, 2-3 seconds down) maximizes muscle engagement and reduces the risk of using momentum.
  • Body positioning: If you feel discomfort in your lower back, try placing a small folded towel under your hips or lower abdomen to reduce excess lordosis (arch) in the spine.
  • Frequency recommendation: As this exercise targets the often-neglected posterior chain, include it 2-3 times per week in your routine for balanced development.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Hyperextending the lower back: Lifting too high and creating excessive arch in the lumbar spine can cause strain or injury. Focus on moderate height with proper muscle engagement.
  • Lifting head too far: Looking up excessively creates cervical spine compression. Keep your gaze toward the floor or slightly forward to maintain a neutral neck position.
  • Using momentum: Jerking or swinging to lift the limbs reduces muscle engagement and increases injury risk. The movement should be slow, controlled, and deliberate.
  • Holding breath: This creates unnecessary internal pressure. Maintain normal breathing throughout the exercise with a focus on exhaling during the most challenging part (the lift).
  • Insufficient duration: Lifting and immediately lowering without a hold at the top position reduces the effectiveness. Aim to hold for 2-3 seconds to maximize muscle activation.
  • Inadequate warm-up: Performing Supermans with a cold, stiff back increases injury risk. Always warm up with gentle movement before attempting this exercise.
  • Lifting arms or legs only: Some beginners lift just the upper or lower body. The simultaneous lifting of both is what makes this exercise effective for balanced posterior chain development.

Superman Variations

Alternating Superman

Lifting opposite arm and leg (right arm with left leg, then left arm with right leg) to reduce lower back strain while still targeting the posterior chain effectively.

Superman Pulses

Once in the elevated position, performing small up-and-down movements (1-2 inches) to increase time under tension and enhance muscle endurance.

Superman with Arm Circles

While maintaining the lifted position, performing small circles with the arms to further engage the shoulders and upper back muscles.

Weighted Superman

Holding light dumbbells (1-3 lbs) in the hands or using ankle weights to increase resistance for advanced practitioners.
