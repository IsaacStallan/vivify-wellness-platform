Close-Grip Bench Press

A powerful compound exercise targeting the triceps while also developing chest and shoulder strength

Triceps, Chest, Shoulders
Intermediate
Barbell, Bench, Rack

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscle: Triceps Brachii (all three heads)
  • Secondary Muscles: Pectoralis Major, Anterior Deltoid
  • Equipment: Barbell, Bench, Rack
  • Type: Compound
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
  • Mechanics: Multi-Joint
  • Force: Push (Horizontal)

How to Perform the Close-Grip Bench Press

  1. 1

    Setup

    Lie flat on a bench with your feet firmly planted on the ground. Set the barbell on the rack at an appropriate height. Your eyes should be directly under the barbell.

  2. 2

    Grip Position

    Grip the barbell with hands placed 8-12 inches apart (approximately shoulder-width or slightly narrower). Your thumbs should be wrapped around the bar for safety (closed grip).

  3. 3

    Unrack the Bar

    Brace your core, squeeze your shoulder blades together, and push upward to unrack the bar. Move the bar horizontally until it's positioned over your mid to lower chest.

  4. 4

    Lower the Bar

    Inhale as you slowly lower the barbell to your lower chest (sternum area), keeping your elbows tucked close to your body at about a 45-degree angle. This elbow position is crucial for triceps engagement.

  5. 5

    Bottom Position

    Allow the bar to lightly touch your chest without bouncing. Your forearms should remain perpendicular to the floor throughout the movement, and your elbows should be close to your sides.

  6. 6

    Press the Bar

    Exhale as you press the barbell upward by extending your elbows. Focus on pushing through your triceps rather than your chest. Maintain the close elbow position throughout the movement.

  7. 7

    Lockout

    Extend your arms fully at the top position, achieving full triceps contraction. Be careful not to hyperextend your elbows. The bar should be directly over your shoulders or mid-chest.

  8. 8

    Repeat & Rack

    Perform the desired number of repetitions with control and proper form. After completing your set, rack the barbell by bringing it horizontally over the rack hooks and carefully lowering it.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Elbow position: Keep your elbows tucked close to your body (approximately 45 degrees from your torso) throughout the entire movement to maximize triceps engagement.
  • Proper grip width: Hands should be placed 8-12 inches apart—too narrow can strain the wrists, while too wide shifts emphasis to the chest.
  • Wrist alignment: Keep your wrists straight (not bent backward) to prevent strain and maintain proper force transfer.
  • Back position: Maintain a slight natural arch in your lower back with your shoulder blades retracted and depressed (pulled down and back).
  • Foot placement: Keep your feet flat on the floor and use leg drive to provide stability throughout the movement.
  • Control the tempo: Lower the bar with control (2-3 seconds down) and press with intent (1-2 seconds up) for optimal muscle development.
  • Breathing pattern: Inhale during the lowering phase and exhale during the pressing phase.
  • Bar path: The bar should travel in a slight diagonal path from your sternum at the bottom to over your mid-chest/shoulders at the top.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Grip too narrow: Placing hands too close together creates excessive wrist strain and limits the amount of weight you can lift safely.
  • Flaring elbows: Allowing elbows to flare out turns the exercise into a regular bench press, reducing triceps involvement and increasing shoulder stress.
  • Bouncing the bar: Using momentum by bouncing the bar off your chest reduces muscle activation and increases injury risk.
  • Lifting hips off bench: Raising your hips changes the angle and reduces the effectiveness of the exercise while potentially straining your lower back.
  • Partial range of motion: Not lowering the bar fully to your chest or not extending fully at the top limits muscle development.
  • Using too much weight: Sacrificing form for heavier weight increases injury risk and reduces triceps activation.
  • Neglecting shoulder stability: Failing to retract and depress your shoulder blades can lead to shoulder impingement and reduced effectiveness.

Close-Grip Bench Press Variations

Dumbbell Close-Grip Bench Press

Using dumbbells with a neutral grip (palms facing each other) for greater range of motion and wrist comfort.

Incline Close-Grip Bench Press

Performed on an incline bench to increase upper chest involvement while still targeting the triceps.

Smith Machine Close-Grip Bench Press

Using a Smith machine for added stability and safety when training without a spotter.

Floor Close-Grip Press

Performed on the floor instead of a bench, limiting the range of motion but increasing stability and safety for beginners.
