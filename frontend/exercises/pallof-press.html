Pallof Press

An anti-rotation core exercise that builds functional stability and trunk control

Core, Obliques, Trunk Stabilizers
Intermediate
Cable Machine or Resistance Band

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscles: Transverse Abdominis, Internal and External Obliques
  • Secondary Muscles: Rectus Abdominis, Erector Spinae, Glutes, Shoulder Stabilizers
  • Equipment: Cable Machine or Resistance Band with Anchor
  • Type: Anti-Rotation, Stability
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
  • Force Type: Anti-Rotation (Isometric)

How to Perform the Pallof Press

  1. 1

    Setup

    Position a cable machine or anchor a resistance band at chest/mid-torso height. Stand perpendicular to the cable or band with your feet shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent. The cable/band should be aligned with the center of your chest.

  2. 2

    Starting Position

    Grasp the handle with both hands and step away from the machine until there's tension in the cable/band. Hold the handle at the center of your chest with elbows bent at about 90 degrees. Your body should be sideways to the cable attachment.

  3. 3

    Core Engagement

    Engage your core by drawing your navel toward your spine and bracing as if preparing to be punched in the stomach. Squeeze your glutes and keep your shoulders pulled back and down.

  4. 4

    Press Phase

    While maintaining a strong, braced core, slowly extend your arms forward until they're fully extended, pressing the handle away from your chest. The key is to resist the rotational force pulling you toward the machine/anchor point.

  5. 5

    Hold

    Hold the extended position for 1-2 seconds, focusing on maintaining perfect alignment—your hips and shoulders should remain square, facing forward, with no rotation in any direction.

  6. 6

    Return Phase

    Slowly bring your hands back to your chest, maintaining the same level of core engagement and body alignment throughout the movement.

  7. 7

    Repeat & Switch Sides

    Complete the desired number of repetitions on one side (typically 8-12), then turn around to perform the same number on the opposite side. The cable/band should pull toward your opposite side for the second set.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Maintain neutral spine: Keep your spine in its natural alignment throughout the movement—no arching or rounding of the back.
  • Resist rotation: The purpose of this exercise is to resist rotation, not to allow it. If you notice your body turning toward the cable/band, reduce the weight/resistance.
  • Breathe properly: Exhale as you press outward and inhale as you return to the starting position, but maintain core tension throughout.
  • Start light: Begin with a lower resistance to master the form before progressing to heavier weights or bands.
  • Engage your glutes: Keeping your glutes contracted helps stabilize your pelvis and prevents compensatory hip movement.
  • Adjust height for variation: Try performing the exercise with the cable/band at different heights (high, mid, low) to target different aspects of your core.
  • Focus on time under tension: Move slowly and deliberately—this is not a speed exercise but one focused on control and stability.
  • Quality over quantity: Fewer, well-executed reps are more effective than many sloppy ones.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rotating your torso: Allowing your body to rotate toward the cable/band defeats the purpose of the exercise. Keep your shoulders and hips squared forward.
  • Holding your breath: Breathing should be controlled but continuous. Holding your breath can cause unnecessary pressure and tension.
  • Raising your shoulders: Keep your shoulders relaxed and down, away from your ears, to prevent unnecessary tension in your upper traps.
  • Using momentum: Avoid using momentum or jerky movements to extend your arms. The movement should be slow and controlled.
  • Insufficient core engagement: The effectiveness of this exercise depends on proper core bracing. Don't focus solely on arm movement while neglecting your core.
  • Excessive weight/resistance: Using too much resistance leads to compensatory movements and poor form. Start lighter and progress gradually.
  • Arching or hunching posture: Maintain a neutral spine position with natural curves—avoid excessive arching or hunching.

Pallof Press Variations

Tall Kneeling Pallof Press

Performed in a kneeling position to remove lower body contribution and place greater emphasis on core stabilization.

Half-Kneeling Pallof Press

Performed with one knee on the ground and the other foot planted, adding an anti-lateral flexion component to the exercise.

Pallof Press with Rotation

After extending arms, rotate slightly away from the cable/band, increasing the anti-rotation challenge and targeting obliques more intensely.

Overhead Pallof Press

Extending arms overhead rather than forward, adding a challenging anti-lateral flexion component and engaging more upper body stabilizers.
