Your Money is Protected

All banks listed here are covered by the Australian Government's Financial Claims Scheme, which protects deposits up to $250,000 per account holder per bank. This means your money is safe regardless of which bank you choose.

Choosing the Right Bank: Expert Tips

Consider Your Lifestyle

If you're a digital native who rarely visits branches, ING or ANZ might be perfect. If you prefer face-to-face support, Commonwealth Bank or Westpac offer the most branches.

Look Beyond Just Fees

While no monthly fees are important, consider ATM access, international fees, savings rates, and customer service. A bank that saves you $3 in ATM fees monthly adds up to $36 annually.

Start With Student Accounts

Most banks offer special student accounts with better terms. You'll typically need to provide your student ID and may need to re-verify your status annually.

Plan for Your Future

Consider which bank offers good graduate programs, business banking (if you're entrepreneurial), or home loans (for future property purchases).

Test the Mobile App

You'll likely do 90% of your banking on your phone. Download the apps and test them before deciding. Look for features like spending tracking and instant notifications.

Don't Feel Locked In

Switching banks is easier than ever in Australia. If your needs change or you find a better offer, you can always switch. Many banks will even help with the transition.

Important Reminders

Always read the full terms and conditions before opening an account. Student benefits may expire after graduation, and fee structures can change. Keep your student ID handy for verification, and remember to update your details if you change address or phone number.

Ready to Open Your Account?

Once you've chosen your bank, gather your documents and apply online or visit a branch to get started.
