Practice real-world social scenarios with expert-designed conversations and Australian cultural context.

Social Events
Networking
Joining a Study Group
Situation: You want to join an existing study group
You: "Hey, I heard you guys have a study group for Marketing. I'm really struggling with the concepts - would it be okay if I joined you?"
Group member: "Yeah, for sure! We meet every Tuesday at 6pm in the library."
You: "That's perfect! I can bring notes from the lecture I attended that you missed last week if that helps."
Key: Show you can contribute value, not just take from the group
Approaching a Lecturer
Situation: Asking for help after class
You: "Professor Smith, could I grab a quick word? I'm having trouble understanding how to apply the framework from today's lecture."
Lecturer: "Of course! What specifically are you struggling with?"
You: "I understand the theory, but I'm not sure how to structure my analysis for the assignment."
Key: Be specific about your question and show you've made an effort to understand
First Day at Work
Situation: Introducing yourself to team members
You: "Hi everyone! I'm Alex, the new marketing intern. I'm really excited to be working with such a talented team."
Team member: "Welcome aboard! What's your background?"
You: "I'm studying marketing at uni and I'm particularly interested in digital strategy. I'd love to learn how you guys approach campaign development."
Key: Show enthusiasm, mention your relevant background, and express interest in learning
Workplace Disagreement
Situation: Respectfully disagreeing with a colleague's approach
Colleague: "I think we should focus all our budget on social media advertising."
You: "That's a strong strategy. I wonder if we could also consider some traditional channels to reach the older demographic in our target market?"
Colleague: "Hmm, that's a good point. What did you have in mind?"
Key: Acknowledge their idea first, then add your perspective as complementary rather than contradictory
Meeting New People at Party
Situation: Starting conversation at a house party
You: "Hey! I'm Sarah. How do you know Tom?"
New person: "Hi Sarah! I'm Jake, we work together. What about you?"
You: "We went to school together. What kind of work do you do? Tom mentioned you guys have some pretty interesting projects."
Key: Start with connection question, then build on their response with genuine interest
Professional Networking Event
Situation: Approaching someone at a networking event
You: "Hi, I'm Emma. This is a great turnout tonight! Are you in marketing as well?"
Professional: "Hey Emma! Yes, I'm in digital marketing at XYZ Company. What about yourself?"
You: "I'm finishing my marketing degree and really interested in the digital side. I'd love to hear about your experience in the industry."
Key: Show genuine interest in their experience and ask thoughtful questions
