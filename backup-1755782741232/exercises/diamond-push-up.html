Diamond Push-Up

A challenging bodyweight exercise that intensely targets the triceps while also engaging the chest and shoulders

Triceps, Chest, Shoulders
Intermediate
Bodyweight

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscle: Triceps Brachii
  • Secondary Muscles: Pectoralis Major, Anterior Deltoid, Core
  • Equipment: None (Bodyweight)
  • Type: Compound
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
  • Mechanics: Multi-Joint
  • Force: Push (Vertical)

How to Perform the Diamond Push-Up

  1. 1

    Starting Position

    Begin in a high plank position with your hands placed directly under your chest, closer than shoulder-width apart. Position your index fingers and thumbs so they touch, forming a diamond or triangle shape. Your body should form a straight line from head to heels.

  2. 2

    Body Alignment

    Engage your core muscles to keep your body rigid and straight. Your shoulders should be directly above your wrists, with your weight distributed evenly between your hands and toes. Keep your neck neutral by looking slightly ahead of you on the floor.

  3. 3

    Hand Position

    Ensure your hands form a proper diamond shape, with your thumbs and index fingers touching to create a triangle or diamond under your chest. This narrow hand position is what shifts the emphasis to your triceps.

  4. 4

    Lowering Phase

    Inhale as you slowly bend your elbows to lower your chest toward your hands. Keep your elbows close to your body rather than flaring them out to the sides. This elbow position is crucial for targeting the triceps effectively.

  5. 5

    Bottom Position

    Lower yourself until your chest nearly touches or lightly touches your hands (the diamond/triangle shape). Your elbows should be pointing backward rather than to the sides, and your body should maintain a straight line from head to heels.

  6. 6

    Pushing Phase

    Exhale as you push through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position. Focus on engaging your triceps to power the movement. Keep your core engaged throughout to maintain proper body alignment.

  7. 7

    Top Position

    At the top of the movement, your arms should be fully extended but not locked, with your body forming a straight line from head to heels. Squeeze your triceps at the top for a moment before beginning the next repetition.

  8. 8

    Repeat

    Perform the desired number of repetitions while maintaining proper form throughout. A typical range for diamond push-ups is 8-15 reps per set, depending on your fitness level.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Elbow position: Keep your elbows close to your body throughout the movement, not flared outward, to maximize triceps engagement.
  • Body alignment: Maintain a straight line from head to heels—avoid sagging hips or raising your buttocks in the air.
  • Hand placement: Position your hands directly under your chest with thumbs and index fingers touching to form the diamond shape.
  • Core engagement: Keep your core muscles engaged throughout the exercise to maintain proper form and protect your lower back.
  • Range of motion: Lower your chest as close to your hands as possible without compromising form for maximum benefit.
  • Breathing pattern: Inhale during the lowering phase and exhale while pushing back up.
  • Controlled movement: Perform the exercise slowly and with control, especially during the lowering phase.
  • Head position: Keep your neck in a neutral position by looking slightly ahead of you on the floor, not up or down.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Flaring elbows: Allowing your elbows to point outward reduces triceps activation and increases shoulder stress.
  • Improper hand position: Placing hands too far apart or too far forward/backward reduces the effectiveness of the exercise.
  • Sagging hips: Allowing your hips to drop compromises proper body alignment and reduces core engagement.
  • Raising buttocks: Lifting your hips too high creates an ineffective angle and reduces the work on your triceps.
  • Incomplete range of motion: Not lowering your chest close enough to your hands limits the benefits of the exercise.
  • Holding breath: Forgetting to breathe properly can limit performance and increase tension.
  • Rushed repetitions: Performing the movements too quickly reduces muscle engagement and can lead to poor form.

Diamond Push-Up Variations

Elevated Feet Diamond Push-Up

Performing the exercise with feet elevated on a bench or stability ball increases difficulty and shifts more weight to the upper body.

Knee Diamond Push-Up

An easier variation performed with knees on the ground, perfect for beginners or those building up strength.

Decline Diamond Push-Up

Performing the exercise with hands on an elevated surface reduces difficulty while maintaining the triceps emphasis.

Weighted Diamond Push-Up

Adding resistance with a weight plate on your back or a weighted vest for increased challenge.
