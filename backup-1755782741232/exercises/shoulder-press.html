Shoulder Press

A fundamental compound exercise for building strong, well-developed shoulders

Shoulders, Triceps
Intermediate
Barbell or Dumbbells

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscle: Deltoids (Shoulders)
  • Secondary Muscles: Triceps, Upper Traps, Upper Chest
  • Equipment: Barbell or Dumbbells
  • Type: Compound
  • Experience Level: Intermediate
  • Mechanics: Push
  • Force: Push (Vertical)

How to Perform the Barbell Shoulder Press

  1. 1

    Starting Position

    Set up a rack with the barbell at upper chest height. Grip the barbell with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Unrack the barbell and step back from the rack. Position your feet shoulder-width apart for stability.

  2. 2

    Proper Stance

    Stand with a straight back, core engaged, and the barbell resting at your upper chest/clavicle area. Your elbows should be pointing downward and slightly forward, not out to the sides.

  3. 3

    Bracing

    Take a deep breath, brace your core, and slightly tighten your glutes to stabilize your entire body before beginning the press.

  4. 4

    The Press

    Press the barbell upward in a slightly arced path, moving your head back slightly to allow the bar to clear your face. Press until your arms are fully extended overhead, with the bar positioned directly above the shoulders.

  5. 5

    Top Position

    At the top, your arms should be fully extended but not forcefully locked out, with the barbell directly over your shoulders and mid-foot. Your body should form a straight line from head to toe.

  6. 6

    Lowering Phase

    Lower the barbell under control back to the starting position at your upper chest/clavicle area, following the same slightly arced path. Move your head forward slightly as the bar descends.

  7. 7

    Repeat

    Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining proper form and core engagement throughout the entire set.

How to Perform the Dumbbell Shoulder Press

  1. 1

    Starting Position

    Sit on a bench with back support or stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height with palms facing forward or facing each other (neutral grip).

  2. 2

    Proper Stance/Posture

    If standing, engage your core and maintain a neutral spine. If seated, press your back against the support and place feet firmly on the floor.

  3. 3

    Bracing

    Take a deep breath and brace your core to stabilize your body before beginning the press.

  4. 4

    The Press

    Press the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended overhead. The dumbbells should move in a slight arc and can either come together at the top (if using forward-facing grip) or remain parallel (if using neutral grip).

  5. 5

    Top Position

    At the top, your arms should be extended but not forcefully locked out, with the dumbbells positioned directly over your shoulders.

  6. 6

    Lowering Phase

    Lower the dumbbells under control back to the starting position at shoulder level, following the same slightly arced path.

  7. 7

    Repeat

    Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining proper form and control throughout the entire set.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Elbow positioning: For optimal shoulder activation, keep your elbows directly under your wrists throughout the movement.
  • Bar path: The bar should follow a slight arc around your face, not straight up and down, to accommodate the natural movement pattern of the shoulders.
  • Head position: Move your head slightly backward during the press and forward during the descent to allow proper bar path while maintaining a neutral spine.
  • Grip width: For barbell presses, a grip slightly wider than shoulder-width typically provides the best balance of shoulder activation and comfort.
  • Core engagement: Keep your core tight throughout the movement to stabilize your spine, especially for standing variations.
  • Wrist alignment: Maintain straight wrists with knuckles pointing toward the ceiling to prevent wrist strain.
  • Full range of motion: Lower the weight to at least chin level (for barbell) or shoulder level (for dumbbells) to get the full benefit of the exercise.
  • Breathing technique: Inhale before pressing, exhale during the press, and inhale during the lowering phase.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Arching the lower back: Excessive arching can lead to back strain. Maintain a neutral spine by engaging your core and slightly tucking your pelvis.
  • Flaring the elbows: Keeping elbows too far out to the sides places excessive stress on the shoulder joints. Keep them in a more natural position, pointing slightly forward.
  • Incomplete range of motion: Not lowering the weight far enough or not fully extending at the top reduces the effectiveness and muscle development.
  • Letting the bar drift forward: The bar should stay in line with your shoulders throughout the movement, not drifting forward which can strain the shoulders.
  • Using momentum: Avoid using leg drive, hip thrust, or back arch to help press the weight. This reduces shoulder muscle activation and increases injury risk.
  • Holding breath: Failing to breathe properly can increase blood pressure and reduce stability. Establish a consistent breathing pattern.
  • Rolling shoulders forward: This reduces shoulder stability and can lead to impingement. Keep your shoulders pulled back and down throughout the movement.

Shoulder Press Variations

Seated Dumbbell Press

Performed seated with back support for maximum stability and isolation of the shoulder muscles.

Push Press

Incorporates a slight leg drive to move heavier weights, building explosive power and strength.

Arnold Press

A dumbbell variation that incorporates rotation, engaging all three heads of the deltoid for complete shoulder development.

Machine Shoulder Press

Provides a guided path of motion, making it easier to learn proper form and ideal for beginners.
