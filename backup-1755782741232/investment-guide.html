Investment Options for Students

High-Interest Savings Account

Low Risk

A safe place to start with guaranteed returns. Perfect for emergency funds and short-term goals.

Key Features:

Expected Return: 4-5% p.a.
Minimum Investment: $1
Liquidity: Instant
Tax Treatment: Taxable

Pros

  • Government guaranteed (up to $250k)
  • No loss of capital
  • Easy access to funds
  • No fees typically

Cons

  • Returns barely beat inflation
  • Interest is fully taxable
  • Opportunity cost vs other investments

Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)

Medium Risk

Diversified funds that track market indices. Ideal first investment for beginners wanting market exposure.

Key Features:

Expected Return: 7-10% p.a.
Minimum Investment: ~$500
Liquidity: Daily
Management Fees: 0.03-0.75%

Pros

  • Instant diversification
  • Low management fees
  • Professional management
  • Easy to buy and sell

Cons

  • Market volatility
  • No guaranteed returns
  • Brokerage fees to buy/sell

Individual Stocks

High Risk

Buying shares in individual companies. Higher potential returns but requires research and carries more risk.

Key Features:

Expected Return: Varies widely
Minimum Investment: ~$500
Liquidity: Daily
Research Required: High

Pros

  • Potential for high returns
  • Dividend income possible
  • Full control over choices
  • Learn about businesses

Cons

  • High risk of loss
  • Requires significant research
  • Lack of diversification
  • Emotional decision making

Term Deposits

Low Risk

Lock away money for a fixed period in exchange for guaranteed higher interest rates than savings accounts.

Key Features:

Expected Return: 4.5-5.5% p.a.
Minimum Investment: $1,000
Term Length: 3-60 months
Early Exit: Penalties apply

Pros

  • Guaranteed returns
  • Government guaranteed (up to $250k)
  • Fixed interest rate
  • Forces you to save

Cons

  • Money locked away
  • Returns may not beat inflation
  • Opportunity cost
  • Interest fully taxable

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

Medium Risk

Invest in property without buying physical real estate. Get exposure to commercial and residential property markets.

Key Features:

Expected Return: 6-9% p.a.
Minimum Investment: ~$500
Liquidity: Daily
Income: Regular distributions

Pros

  • Property exposure without buying
  • Regular income distributions
  • Professional management
  • Diversification across properties

Cons

  • Sensitive to interest rates
  • Property market cycles
  • Management fees
  • Tax complexity

Government Bonds

Low Risk

Lend money to the Australian government in exchange for regular interest payments and return of capital.

Key Features:

Expected Return: 3-5% p.a.
Minimum Investment: $100
Term: 1-30 years
Default Risk: Virtually zero

Pros

  • Government backed security
  • Predictable income
  • Capital preservation
  • Portfolio diversification

Cons

  • Low returns vs inflation
  • Interest rate risk
  • Limited growth potential
  • Opportunity cost

Superannuation: Your Retirement Foundation

Understanding super is crucial for Australian students. Your employer must contribute 11% of your salary to super, and you can add more for huge tax benefits.

Employer Contributions

Your employer must contribute 11% of your salary to superannuation (increasing to 12% by 2025). This is on top of your wage.

Example:

Salary: $25,000
Super: $2,750 automatically
Total package: $27,750

Salary Sacrifice

Contribute extra to super from your pre-tax salary. Only pay 15% tax instead of your marginal tax rate.

Tax Saving:

$1,000 salary sacrifice
Tax saved: $40-300
Depending on your tax rate

Co-Contributions

Government matches up to $500 of personal super contributions if you earn under $42,016 and contribute after-tax money.

Free Money:

You contribute: $1,000
Government adds: $500
50% instant return!

First Home Super Saver

Save for your first home through super. Withdraw up to $50,000 of contributions plus earnings with tax benefits.

Benefits:

Lower tax on contributions
Investment growth
Faster home deposit saving

Important Super Rules

You generally can't access super until age 60-65, but there are exceptions like the First Home Super Saver Scheme and severe financial hardship. Choose your super fund wisely - fees can cost you tens of thousands over your career.

Smart Investment Strategies for Students

Dollar-Cost Averaging

Invest the same amount regularly regardless of market conditions. This reduces the impact of market volatility and removes emotion from investing.

How to implement:

  1. Choose an amount you can invest monthly (e.g., $200)
  2. Set up automatic transfers to your brokerage account
  3. Buy the same investment every month
  4. Don't try to time the market
  5. Stick to the plan for years

Core-Satellite Approach

Build a core portfolio of diversified, low-cost index funds (80-90%) with smaller satellite positions in specific sectors or stocks (10-20%).

Portfolio example:

  1. Core: 60% Australian index ETF (VAS)
  2. Core: 30% International index ETF (VGS)
  3. Satellite: 5% Emerging markets ETF
  4. Satellite: 5% Individual growth stocks
  5. Rebalance annually

The Three-Fund Portfolio

Ultra-simple diversification with just three funds: Australian shares, international shares, and bonds. Perfect for beginners.

Allocation example:

  1. 40% Australian shares (VAS or A200)
  2. 40% International shares (VGS or VDHG)
  3. 20% Australian bonds (VAF or VGB)
  4. Adjust based on age and risk tolerance
  5. Rebalance every 6-12 months

Emergency Fund First

Before investing in growth assets, build an emergency fund of 3-6 months expenses in a high-interest savings account.

Priority order:

  1. Pay off high-interest debt (credit cards)
  2. Save $1,000 starter emergency fund
  3. Get employer super match (if applicable)
  4. Build full emergency fund (3-6 months)
  5. Start investing in growth assets

Age-Based Asset Allocation

Adjust your risk level based on your age. Younger investors can take more risk for higher long-term returns.

Simple rule of thumb:

  1. Age 20-30: 90-100% growth assets
  2. Age 30-40: 80-90% growth assets
  3. Age 40-50: 70-80% growth assets
  4. Age 50-60: 60-70% growth assets
  5. Review and adjust every 5 years

Tax-Efficient Investing

Minimize taxes on your investments by using tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investment choices.

Tax strategies:

  1. Maximize super contributions first
  2. Hold growth assets for >12 months (50% CGT discount)
  3. Use franking credits from Australian shares
  4. Consider tax-loss harvesting
  5. Choose tax-efficient ETFs over managed funds

Investment Tips for Student Success

Start with Index Funds

Begin with broad market index funds like VAS (Australian shares) or VGS (international shares). They're diversified, low-cost, and require no stock picking skills.

Automate Everything

Set up automatic transfers to your investment account and automatic purchases. This removes emotion and ensures you invest consistently regardless of market conditions.

Don't Try to Time the Market

Even professionals struggle to time the market. Instead, invest regularly and stay invested for the long term. Time in the market beats timing the market.

Keep Costs Low

High fees compound negatively over time. Choose low-cost ETFs (under 0.5% management fees) and discount brokers to maximize your returns.

Diversify Globally

Don't put all your money in Australian shares. Include international exposure to reduce risk and capture global growth opportunities.

Stay Educated

Read books like "The Barefoot Investor" and "A Random Walk Down Wall Street." Follow reputable financial education sources and never stop learning.

Ignore Market Noise

Daily market movements are irrelevant for long-term investors. Focus on your goals, not headlines. Consider limiting how often you check your portfolio.

Maximize Super First

Before investing in taxable accounts, consider maximizing super contributions. The tax benefits and government co-contributions can provide immediate returns.

Investment Warnings

Never invest money you can't afford to lose. Investments can go down as well as up, and past performance doesn't guarantee future returns. Avoid get-rich-quick schemes, day trading, and investing in things you don't understand. Always do your own research and consider seeking professional advice for complex situations.
