Hammer Curl

Build stronger, more functional arms by targeting the brachialis and improving forearm development

Arms, Brachialis, Forearms
Beginner
Dumbbells

Exercise Overview

Exercise Details

  • Primary Muscle: Brachialis, Brachioradialis
  • Secondary Muscles: Biceps Brachii, Forearm Flexors
  • Equipment: Dumbbells
  • Type: Isolation
  • Experience Level: Beginner
  • Mechanics: Single Joint
  • Force: Pull (Elbow Flexion)

Video Demonstration

How to Perform the Hammer Curl

  1. 1

    Starting Position

    Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with your arms fully extended by your sides and palms facing your body (neutral grip). Keep your shoulders back and chest up.

  2. 2

    Brace Your Core

    Engage your core muscles to maintain a neutral spine throughout the exercise. Your upper arms should remain close to your sides during the entire movement.

  3. 3

    Curl Phase

    Keeping your elbows fixed at your sides, exhale as you curl the dumbbells upward by flexing at the elbows. Maintain the neutral grip (thumbs pointing up) throughout the movement.

  4. 4

    Top Position

    Continue curling until the dumbbells reach shoulder height and your biceps are fully contracted. The dumbbells should be vertical at the top position, with your thumbs pointing toward your shoulders.

  5. 5

    Peak Contraction

    At the top of the movement, squeeze your arms for a brief pause to maximize contraction in the brachialis and forearm muscles.

  6. 6

    Lowering Phase

    Inhale as you slowly lower the weights back to the starting position in a controlled manner. Maintain tension in your arms throughout the descent.

  7. 7

    Repeat

    Perform the desired number of repetitions while maintaining proper form. A typical range for hammer curls is 10-15 reps per set.

Form & Technique Tips

  • Wrist position: Keep your wrists neutral (straight) throughout the exercise. Avoid flexing or extending your wrists to prevent strain.
  • Elbow placement: Keep your elbows pinned to your sides. They should function as hinges and not move forward or outward.
  • Control the movement: Avoid using momentum or swinging to lift the weights. The movement should come solely from your arm muscles.
  • Full range of motion: Allow your arms to fully extend at the bottom and fully contract at the top for maximum benefit.
  • Maintain posture: Keep your back straight, shoulders back, and head neutral. Avoid arching your back or leaning backward.
  • Breathing pattern: Exhale during the curl (lifting phase) and inhale during the lowering phase.
  • Equal focus: Pay attention to both arms to ensure balanced development. Don't let your stronger arm compensate for the weaker one.
  • Appropriate weight: Choose a weight that allows you to maintain proper form throughout all repetitions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using momentum: Swinging the weights up with body movement decreases muscle engagement and increases injury risk.
  • Changing grip mid-set: Rotating your wrists during the movement changes the exercise and reduces focus on the target muscles.
  • Moving the elbows: Allowing your elbows to drift away from your sides shifts emphasis to different muscles and reduces effectiveness.
  • Excessive weight: Using too heavy weights forces you to break form and reduces isolation of the target muscles.
  • Incomplete range of motion: Not fully extending the arms at the bottom or not bringing the weights high enough reduces the effectiveness of the exercise.
  • Poor posture: Slouching or excessively arching the back can lead to back strain and compromises the exercise benefits.
  • Rushing the movement: Performing reps too quickly reduces time under tension and diminishes muscle development.

Hammer Curl Variations

Alternating Hammer Curls

Perform the movement one arm at a time, alternating between right and left for better focus on each side.

Seated Hammer Curls

Performed while seated to eliminate lower body involvement and increase focus on proper upper body form.

Cross-Body Hammer Curls

Curl the dumbbells toward the opposite shoulder to increase biceps peak contraction and add variety.

Incline Hammer Curls

Performed on an incline bench to increase the stretch on the biceps and brachialis at the bottom of the movement.
